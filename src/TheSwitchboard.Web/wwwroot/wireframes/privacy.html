Privacy Policy

Effective April 16, 2026

This Privacy Policy describes how The Switchboard, LLC (“The Switchboard,” “we,” or “us”) collects, uses, and protects information when you visit theswitchboardmarketing.com (the “Site”). This Site is a corporate marketing presence. Our product platform is governed by a separate agreement with each customer.

1. Information We Collect

Information you provide

When you contact us through a form on the Site, we collect the information you submit — typically your name, business email, company, role, and the message you send. Providing this information is voluntary and only occurs when you initiate contact with us.

Information collected automatically

When you visit the Site, our servers and our proprietary first-party analytics tool record limited technical information to operate and improve the Site, including:

  • IP address and approximate geographic region
  • Browser type, device type, and operating system
  • Pages viewed, referring URL, and time spent on the Site
  • Session identifiers used to distinguish one visit from another

We do not use Google Analytics, Meta Pixel, LinkedIn Insight Tag, or any other third-party advertising or analytics trackers on this Site. Our analytics are operated in-house.

2. How We Use Information

We use the information described above to:

  • Respond to inquiries and continue conversations you have initiated
  • Understand how visitors engage with the Site and improve its content and performance
  • Protect the Site against abuse, fraud, and security threats
  • Meet legal, regulatory, and recordkeeping obligations

We do not sell your information. We do not share it with advertising networks or data brokers.

3. How We Share Information

We share information only with a small set of service providers that help us operate the Site and our business. These providers are bound by confidentiality obligations and may only use information as needed to perform services for us. Current service providers include:

  • Our cloud hosting provider, for Site delivery
  • Our transactional email provider, to deliver replies to inquiries
  • Our internal customer relationship system, to track conversations we have initiated

We may also disclose information if required by law, in response to valid legal process, or to protect the rights, property, or safety of The Switchboard or others.

4. Cookies and Similar Technologies

The Site uses a small number of first-party cookies and similar technologies for essential functions such as remembering session state and supporting our own analytics. We do not place third-party advertising cookies. Most browsers allow you to refuse or delete cookies through settings. Refusing cookies may affect Site functionality but will not prevent you from viewing public pages.

5. Data Retention

We retain information for as long as reasonably necessary to fulfill the purposes described in this Policy, comply with our legal obligations, resolve disputes, and enforce our agreements. Contact-form submissions are retained until the conversation is closed and then archived according to our internal retention schedule.

6. Data Security

We maintain administrative, technical, and physical safeguards designed to protect information against unauthorized access, loss, misuse, or alteration. No method of transmission over the internet is completely secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.

7. Your Rights

Depending on where you live, you may have rights regarding the personal information we hold about you, including the right to access, correct, delete, or restrict the use of that information, and the right to object to certain processing. To exercise any of these rights, please contact us using the information below. We will verify your request and respond within the time required by applicable law.

California residents have additional rights under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA). We do not sell personal information as that term is defined under the CCPA.

8. Children’s Privacy

The Site is directed to business professionals and is not intended for children under the age of 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If you believe a child has provided information to us, please contact us and we will delete it.

9. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. When we do, we will revise the “Effective” date at the top of this page. Material changes will be communicated through a notice on the Site. Your continued use of the Site after any change constitutes your acceptance of the updated Policy.

10. Contact Us

If you have questions about this Privacy Policy or how we handle information, please reach out.
