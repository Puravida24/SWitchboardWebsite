Editorial · Vol. I

Insurance is not a data problem.

It is a judgment business — and the gap between marketing and underwriting has always been the most expensive seat in the house.

Every carrier, every agency, every MGA operates in a world where the difference between a profitable book and a failing one comes down to knowing which accounts deserve attention — and which do not. The cost of that judgment, compounded across a year of inbound, is the difference between loss ratios that make the quarterly review and ones that do not.

We created The Switchboard because we have sat on both sides of the table — writing insurance AND funding the demand that feeds it. From that seat, the gap between marketing and underwriting was never theoretical. It was the line item that decided whether a campaign paid for itself or quietly bled the book.

So we closed it. What you see here is the intelligence layer we wished we had, built by the people who knew exactly what was missing — and exactly what it cost to not have it.

The Intelligence

Five moves, in order, every time.

What happens to every inbound record between the source and the underwriter — in under five milliseconds, without a human in the loop.

01

Enrich.

Every inbound record is instantly augmented with firmographic, demographic, and behavioral signal. The full picture — before the first conversation.

02

Score.

Proprietary models, grounded in real insurance outcomes — not marketing proxies — assign a precision score. High-value rises. Noise falls away.

03

Segment.

Records auto-group by line of business, geography, policy type, and predicted lifetime value. Your team works the right book, not all of it.

04

Route.

Each scored, segmented prospect lands with the right person at the right time. No manual sorting. No cherry-picking. No wasted motion.

05

Analyze.

Real-time dashboards reveal which sources, segments, and producers convert — and which do not. The data loop closes. Strategy sharpens every cycle.
