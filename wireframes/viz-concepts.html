Visualization Concepts · Vol. I

Five ways to make the intelligence layer feel alive.

Exploratory visual directions for the Live Ops section — each shown live, each a different angle on the same story: signal in, decisions out, in real time.

01

Trading floor ticker.

Horizontal ticker strip scrolling across the top (or bottom) of the hero. Account IDs, dollar values, and routing decisions fly past like a Bloomberg terminal. Cheap to build, high "always-on" energy — ties straight to the insurance-as-trading-floor metaphor.

02

Sparkline metrics strip.

Four compact tiles with embedded mini-charts. Each tile shows a headline KPI (Match Rate, Latency, Queue, Uptime) plus its trailing 24-hour trend as a one-line chart. Compact, data-dense, very "institutional dashboard" — works as a standalone section or as an accent under the terminal.

03

Routing flow diagram.

A node-and-edge graph of the intelligence pipeline: Sources → Enrichment → Scoring → Routing → Destinations. Animated packets travel each edge, so you literally see a record flow through the system in real time. This is the most "switchboard-metaphor-literal" option.

Metaphor-native · Editorial
04

Live score distribution.

A histogram of risk scores across the last hour's inbound, bucketed in 100-point bins from 500 to 1000. Bars pulse and re-height as new records arrive. The high-value (800+) buckets light green — the "worth-underwriting" zone. Turns the abstract idea of "scoring" into something you can see.

05

Geographic pulse map.

A stylized US map with pulsing dots where records are being processed right now. Each pulse represents a scored record; size and color indicate value and routing decision. Great for carriers with multi-state footprints — instantly communicates "we work everywhere you do."
